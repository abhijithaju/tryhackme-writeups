Summary
Covered the blue team side of cybersecurity — monitoring, detecting, 
and responding to attacks rather than performing them.

Key areas covered

SOC (Security Operations Center)
A dedicated team that monitors networks and systems around the clock 
for suspicious or malicious activity. Uses tools like SIEM (Security 
Information and Event Management) to collect and analyze logs from 
across the network.

Threat Intelligence
The process of gathering information about potential attackers and 
their methods in order to anticipate and prepare for attacks before 
they happen.

Digital Forensics and Incident Response (DFIR)
Digital Forensics involves investigating systems after an attack to 
determine what happened, how access was gained, and what was 
affected. Incident Response is the structured process of containing 
and recovering from an active attack.

Malware Analysis
Examining malicious software to understand its behavior, purpose, 
and how it spreads — used to build better defenses.

Concepts learned
- Red team attacks, blue team defends and investigates
- SOC teams use SIEM tools to detect threats in real time
- Forensics and incident response are activated after a breach occurs
- Threat intelligence helps organizations stay ahead of attackers
